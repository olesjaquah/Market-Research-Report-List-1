<p><p>The non-agriculture smart irrigation system market primarily consists of two types: weather-based controllers and sensor-based controllers. Weather-based controllers utilize local climate data to adjust irrigation schedules, ensuring efficient water use by responding to changing weather conditions. In contrast, sensor-based controllers rely on real-time soil moisture and plant health data to optimize irrigation levels, promoting sustainability. Both types enhance water conservation, improve crop health, and increase efficiency, making them essential for modern irrigation practices in non-agricultural settings.</p></p>
