<p><p>The permanent flame retardant fiber market is categorized into staple fiber and long fiber. Staple fibers are short-length strands, commonly used in textiles like apparel and home furnishings, providing comfort and protection from flames. Long fibers, on the other hand, are continuous strands typically utilized in industrial applications, offering enhanced durability and resistance. Both types are engineered to maintain their flame-retardant properties even after multiple washes, making them crucial for safety across various sectors, including aerospace, automotive, and construction.</p></p>
