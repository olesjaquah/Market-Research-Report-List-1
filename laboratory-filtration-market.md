<p><p>The laboratory filtration market encompasses various membrane filtration techniques, including ultrafiltration, microfiltration, nanofiltration, and reverse osmosis. Microfiltration is used for removing larger particles and microorganisms, while ultrafiltration targets smaller solutes and macromolecules. Nanofiltration acts on ions and small organic molecules, providing selective separation. Reverse osmosis, the most advanced technique, effectively removes dissolved salts and contaminants. Each type plays a critical role in diverse applications such as water purification, biotechnology, and pharmaceuticals, catering to specific filtration needs in laboratory settings.</p></p>
