<p><p>The 3D printing photopolymer market is categorized into three types based on the wavelength of light used for curing materials. Ultraviolet (UV) photopolymers are the most common, utilizing UV light to rapidly harden resins. Visible light photopolymers use specific wavelengths in the visible spectrum for curing, offering advantages like faster printing speeds and compatibility with various light sources. Infrared photopolymers, while less common, use infrared light to activate the curing process, enabling novel applications in specific industries.</p></p>
